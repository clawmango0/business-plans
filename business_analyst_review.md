# Business Analyst Review: Fort Worth Ventures

## Executive Summary

This analysis evaluates 7 business opportunities in the Fort Worth, TX market using standardized criteria: market opportunity, competitive landscape, financial viability, operational complexity, and scalability potential.

---

## Evaluation Framework

Each business is scored on 10-point scale across 5 dimensions:

| Dimension | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| Market Opportunity | 25% | Demand, growth trends, underserved segments |
| Competitive Landscape | 20% | Barriers to entry, competitor weakness |
| Financial Viability | 25% | Startup cost, ROI, break-even timeline |
| Operational Complexity | 15% | Skill requirements, equipment, staffing |
| Scalability | 15% | Growth potential, replicability |

---

## Detailed Analysis

### 1. PRESSURE WASHING

**Market Opportunity: 8/10**
- Fort Worth has 1M+ homes requiring exterior cleaning
- High humidity = mold/algae growth = recurring demand
- HOA communities drive consistent maintenance needs
- Seasonal but predictable (spring/fall peak)

**Competitive Landscape: 7/10**
- Fragmented market: mix of solo operators and franchises
- Low barriers to entry = competition
- Weak digital presence among competitors = opportunity

**Financial Viability: 9/10**
- Low startup ($3,900-11,800)
- Fast break-even (2-3 months)
- High margins (60%+ after expenses)
- Excellent cash flow

**Operational Complexity: 8/10**
- Physical labor (moderate demand)
- Equipment: gas pressure washer ($2,500-4,000)
- Can start solo

**Scalability: 6/10**
- Labor-intensive limits growth
- Can add employees, but managing crews is challenging
- Geographic constraint (service area)

**TOTAL: 7.7/10**

---

### 2. TRUCK RENTAL (Work Truck Niche)

**Market Opportunity: 8/10**
- DFW construction industry growing 8% annually
- Fleet downtime = consistent demand
- Untapped niche: "work truck replacement"

**Competitive Landscape: 6/10**
- Enterprise/Hertz don't serve this segment
- Turo inconsistent, high fees
- No focused contractor accounts

**Financial Viability: 5/10**
- High startup ($91K-130K)
- Insurance is major cost ($2,800-5,200/truck/year)
- Need 40%+ utilization to break even

**Operational Complexity: 4/10**
- Vehicle maintenance
- Insurance management
- Damage liability

**Scalability: 7/10**
- Asset-based (trucks retain value)
- Can add trucks predictably
- B2B contracts provide stability

**TOTAL: 6.0/10**

---

### 3. CAR RENTAL

**Market Opportunity: 7/10**
- DFW Airport: 4th busiest globally
- Large local market (car in shop, events)

**Competitive Landscape: 4/10**
- Saturated market
- Enterprise, Hertz, Turo dominate
- Hard to compete on price

**Financial Viability: 5/10**
- Similar costs to truck rental
- Lower daily rates than trucks
- High insurance burden

**Operational Complexity: 4/10**
- Vehicle turnover
- Cleaning between rentals
- Damage management

**Scalability: 5/10**
- Competitive moat is difficult to build

**TOTAL: 5.0/10**
